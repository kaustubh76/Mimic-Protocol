# Envio Growth Engineer — Video Script

**Format:** Loom-style screen-share + talking-head, ~4 minutes
**Audience:** the Envio team, sent privately alongside the four-document package
**Voice:** operator-confident — crisp, numbers-forward, peer tone
**Hook:** "Here's the math behind why this role pays for itself."
**Why this exists:** to compress the strategy + math memos into one watchable artifact the team can absorb in 4 minutes, and to demonstrate I can actually produce the kind of customer-voice content I'm proposing as Play 3.

---

## Production notes (read before recording)

- **Tools:** Loom or any screen-recorder with webcam overlay. Webcam in top-right corner, ~20% screen size. Don't hide your face — the talking head is what makes this not feel like a slideshow.
- **Audio:** wired headset mic or USB condenser. Phone mic is acceptable if it's quiet. **Do not record with laptop built-in mic** — it's the single biggest tell of "I rushed this."
- **Background:** clean wall, neutral light, no clutter. Daytime natural light beats any ring light.
- **Wardrobe:** plain solid-colour shirt or hoodie. No logo'd merch (avoids looking like an outsider performing belonging).
- **Take strategy:** record in 3 segments — Cold Open / Math / Close — then stitch. Don't try to nail 4 minutes in one take. If a segment goes off, restart only that segment.
- **Pace target:** ~150 words per minute. Roughly **600 words total** for a 4-minute video. The script below is paced for that.
- **Total prep time:** 90 minutes including 2–3 takes per segment. Don't over-rehearse; on the 4th take you start sounding scripted.

## Segment 1 — Cold Open (0:00–0:25, ~60 words)

**Visual:** Full-frame webcam. Black title slide visible behind/around you with text: *"The Math Behind a Growth Role at Envio."* No music. Just you, talking.

**Talk track (deliver crisply, eye contact, no smile yet):**

> "Hi, I'm Kaustubh. You've already read the strategy memo and the math memo I sent over. This 4-minute video isn't a re-pitch of those documents — it's the version where I walk you through the math live, on screen, so you can see the model the way I'd present it in our discussion.
>

**Direction note.** Don't introduce yourself for 30 seconds. The team knows who you are. Skip the niceties — *the first sentence has to earn the next 4 minutes of their attention.*

**Direction note.** This is the hardest segment to deliver crisply. Don't read the numbers — *speak* them like you're explaining to a colleague. Scroll *while* talking, not before each block. If you fluff a number, restart that line — but don't restart the whole segment. The math memo is what they're seeing; you're just narrating.

## What NOT to do in the video

- **Don't mention competitors by name.** Same rule as the docs. If a comparison slips in, edit it out.
- **Don't smile-and-wave at the camera in the cold open.** Skip the niceties — your first sentence has to earn the next 4 minutes.
- **Don't read from the script.** Memorize the *structure* and the *numbers*. Speak the words. If you read, the team will hear it.
- **Don't add background music.** This is a peer-to-peer technical conversation, not a YouTube ad.
- **Don't apologize for anything** — pacing, the camera setup, the depth. Apologies in videos read as candidate-tone.
- **Don't end with "thanks for watching."** End with the ask. Close hard.
- **Don't post this publicly.** Privately to the team, alongside the docs. The strategy memo and math memo are the reading; this is the watching.

## How to send it

When the video is recorded:

1. Upload to Loom (private link, no password — friction kills view rate)
2. Set the thumbnail to a frame from Segment 4 — the math memo on screen with your face in the corner. That's the most signal-rich preview frame.
3. Send the link with a one-line message: *"Companion to the docs. 4 minutes. — Kaustubh"*

That one-line message is doing real work. It signals respect for their time, sets the runtime expectation, and matches the operator-confident voice of the video. Don't write three paragraphs of preamble.
